Keeping your journal

Keeping track of new information

New information such as bits of code that are new to you
with a description of what the code is doing. This is where you
would also want to take special note of the formatting requirements.
You will want to label each part of formatting and how it is used.

Sketches and drawings

Drawing out a picture of how your code is supposed to work will be a
critical step. This can take the form of Pseudo_code, which is just writing
out a description, in regular language, of what you want the code to do. Another
thing here is an actual picture that you create. This might take the form of a
flowchart for code or a wiring diagram or even a drawing of how some hardware is
going to be put together.

Pictures or drawings to help you or someone else understand your project are vital.

Keeping Track of Mistakes

Yes, even your mistakes. The important thing here is that you keep track of the steps you take to put things right. What worked and what didn't? If you still haven't found a solution, think about what steps you will take next and put those in your journal with a little heading like Next Steps:

Keep track of resources

Resource can be anything from online documentation to other notes Keep track of websites that have important code snippets or other information that you might need to access at a later date.
